# Real-World Value-Add Apartment Example

## The Below-Market Apartment Turnaround

### Scenario Overview
A realistic value-add investment demonstrating how fixing up a property and bringing rents to market creates substantial equity through NOI growth and cap rate relationships.

### Property Details
- **Property:** 24-unit apartment building in growing suburban market
- **Purchase Price:** $2,400,000 
- **Market Cap Rate:** 7.5%
- **Strategy:** Renovate units during natural tenant turnover over 3 years

### The Opportunity
- **Current Rents:** $800/month per unit (long-term owner kept rents low)
- **Market Rents:** $1,200/month per unit
- **Gap:** $400/month per unit = $115,200 annual upside potential

---

## Year-by-Year Analysis

### Year 0 (Purchase)
**Current State:**
- Rent: $800/unit × 24 units = $19,200/month
- Gross Annual Income: $230,400
- Vacancy/Collection Loss (5%): ($11,520)
- Effective Gross Income: $218,880
- Operating Expenses (36%): ($78,880)
- **Net Operating Income: $140,000**
- **Property Value (7.5% cap): $1,866,667**

*Note: Purchased for $2,400,000 - paying premium for potential!*

### Year 1 (8 Units Renovated)
**Progress:**
- 8 units renovated to $1,200/month
- 16 units remain at $800/month
- Renovation Cost: $8,000/unit × 8 = $64,000

**Financial Performance:**
- Mixed Rent Income: (8 × $1,200 × 12) + (16 × $800 × 12) = $268,800
- Vacancy Loss (5%): ($13,440)
- Effective Income: $255,360
- Operating Expenses: ($85,360)
- **Net Operating Income: $170,000**
- **Property Value (7.5% cap): $2,266,667**

*Equity Created Year 1: $266,667 - $64,000 renovation = $202,667 net*

### Year 2 (16 Units Total Renovated)
**Progress:**
- 16 units now at $1,200/month
- 8 units remain at $800/month
- Additional Renovation Cost: $8,000 × 8 = $64,000

**Financial Performance:**
- Mixed Rent Income: (16 × $1,200 × 12) + (8 × $800 × 12) = $307,200
- Vacancy Loss (5%): ($15,360)
- Effective Income: $291,840
- Operating Expenses: ($91,840)
- **Net Operating Income: $200,000**
- **Property Value (7.5% cap): $2,666,667**

*Additional Equity Year 2: $400,000 - $64,000 renovation = $336,000 net*

### Year 3 (All 24 Units Renovated)
**Completion:**
- All 24 units now at market rent: $1,200/month
- Final Renovation Cost: $8,000 × 8 = $64,000

**Financial Performance:**
- Market Rent Income: 24 × $1,200 × 12 = $345,600
- Vacancy Loss (5%): ($17,280)
- Effective Income: $328,320
- Operating Expenses: ($98,320)
- **Net Operating Income: $230,000**
- **Property Value (7.5% cap): $3,066,667**

*Additional Equity Year 3: $400,000 - $64,000 renovation = $336,000 net*

## Investment Summary

### Total Investment
- Initial Purchase: $2,400,000
- Total Renovations: $192,000 (24 units × $8,000)
- **Total Invested: $2,592,000**

### Returns
- **Final Property Value: $3,066,667**
- **Total Equity Created: $474,667**
- **Return on Investment: 18.3%**
- **Annual NOI Growth: From $140,000 to $230,000 (+$90,000)**

### Value Creation Breakdown
- **NOI Increase:** $90,000
- **Value Increase:** $90,000 ÷ 7.5% = $1,200,000
- **Less Renovation Costs:** ($192,000)
- **Net Value Created:** $1,008,000
- **Less Purchase Premium:** ($533,333)
- **Net Equity Gain:** $474,667

## Key Educational Takeaways

### 1. The NOI-to-Value Connection
- **Formula:** Every $10,000 NOI increase = $133,333 value increase (at 7.5% cap)
- **Multiplier Effect:** $1 of NOI improvement = $13.33 of property value
- **Demonstrates:** Why investors focus intensely on income optimization

### 2. Market Cap Rates Drive Value
- **Consistency:** Same 7.5% cap rate used throughout
- **Market Driven:** Cap rate comes from comparable sales, not investor wishes
- **Value Creation:** Comes from operations, not cap rate compression

### 3. Execution Risk and Rewards
- **Initial "Overpay":** $2.4M purchase vs $1.87M current value
- **Execution Required:** Must successfully renovate and lease up
- **Real Costs:** $192,000 in renovation expenses
- **Time Factor:** 3-year business plan execution

### 4. Value-Add Strategy Fundamentals
- **Identify Gap:** Below-market rents = opportunity
- **Natural Turnover:** No forced evictions, work with lease expirations
- **Reasonable Costs:** $8,000/unit renovation (new kitchen, bath, flooring, paint)
- **Market Validation:** $1,200 rents supported by comparable properties

### 5. Wealth Building Mechanics
- **Forced Appreciation:** Created through operational improvements
- **Compound Effect:** Higher NOI + stable cap rate = exponential value growth
- **Equity Without Sale:** Value increases whether you sell or hold
- **Refinancing Opportunity:** Can potentially pull out equity via refinance

## Risk Considerations

### Market Risks
- Market rents could decline
- Cap rates could increase (reducing value multiplier)
- Competition could limit rent growth

### Execution Risks
- Renovation costs could exceed budget
- Extended vacancy during renovations
- Difficulty achieving projected rents
- Tenant turnover might be slower than anticipated

### Financial Risks
- Interest rate increases affecting financing
- Higher than expected operating expenses
- Need for additional capital improvements
- Vacancy rates higher than projected
